About Me

My love of food was kindled from a young age. I fondly recall hours spent watching my parents cook in the kitchen and yearning for an early taste of what would be served for dinner that day. However, my fascination with cooking was ignited in high school, when I worked as a sushi chef part-time in a local Japanese-American fusion restaurant. From stumbling into the restaurant at 7AM on Saturdays to prepare 50 lb of sushi rice to spending the next 7 hours actually preparing the sushi, the experience not only taught me perseverence and discipline, but also cultivated my passion. I would spend the next year and a half honing my skills and learning how to cook a variety of other Asian fusion dishes.

Now in college, I no longer enjoy the luxury of a having an industrial kitchen at my disposal, but still enjoy cooking and baking in my free time. For me, it serves as not only a way to practice and further develop my skills, but also as a pastime to enjoy with friends. I occassionally post on my food instagram. and intend to use this page as an avenue to post recipes that I've had much success with in the past as well as commentary on what I found to be useful while following them.

姜葱鱼片 (Spring Onion Ginger Fish Fillets)

Tender, flavourful and savoury, this is definitely a childhood favourite of mine! I've found that steaming in a bamboo steamer with some cheesecloth instead of pan-frying them led to a more tender and flakier texture. My family has always made this dish with strips of carrots and sugar snap peas to add more colour, texture and flavour.
